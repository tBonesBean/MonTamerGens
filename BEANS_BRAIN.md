The mega 'dense' lore vibe from the types in **Power** and **Moral** (Hegemon, Paragon, Heretic, Zealot, Emissar, Ordained) is palpable...

Possibly through rigorous trials meant to prove worthiness. The mental model I keep coming back to when trying to define this is: - However a Mon ends up attaining these 'alignments', I've not considered yet. But gaining these 'Orders' are what makes an already rare Machamp Pokemon Card, a fucking **HOLOGRAPHIC** Machamp Pokemon Card. :) they aren't types. Not at all. Too strong and too rich to come as a built-in, "accessories included", Toys'R'Us shopping spree, Monster feature.

### **2. The "Physics Cluster" (Dynamic & Structural)**

- _Observation:_ These are clean, physics-based metaphors. They work best as the abstract types foundation because they describe _how the monster exists in space-time_.

### **3. The "Adjective Cluster" (State & Role)**

- _Observation:_ These now seem to be perfect for future systems that have the potential to achieve a sort of _resolve_ or _absolution_. Triggering a form of progression, or elevation.

### **4. The "Mystery Cluster" (Abstract & Residual)**

- _Observation:_ These are great for evoking a sense of wonder, the unknown, and the metaphysical. They work well to describe _the unseen forces_ that shape the monster's existence.
- _Now Apparent:_ These types could be used to describe _hidden attributes_ or _latent abilities_ that are not immediately visible but influence the monster's behavior and interactions.
